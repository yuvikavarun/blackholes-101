# Anatomy of a Black Hole

<img width="1600" height="900" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d57dc814-857d-4bb8-9565-48355327ad50" />

- **Singularity:** The absolute center. All the black hole's mass is crushed into an infinitely dense point where our current laws of physics completely break down.
- **Event Horizon:** The "point of no return." The invisible spherical boundary around the singularity where the escape velocity becomes faster than the speed of light.
- **Event Horizon Shadow:** The dark central circle we see in telescope images. It is the silhouette caused by the black hole swallowing the light from the glowing gas situated behind it.
- **Photon Sphere:** A thin ring of light just outside the event horizon where gravity is so perfectly balanced that light particles (photons) get caught orbiting the black hole in continuous circles.
- **Accretion Disk:** A super-heated, flattened disk of doomed gas and dust spiraling into the black hole. Friction heats it to millions of degrees, making it glow brilliantly.
- **Doppler Beaming:** An optical illusion caused by the accretion disk spinning near the speed of light. The side spinning toward us appears much brighter than the side spinning away from us.
- **Corona:** A region of ultra-hot, highly energetic particles hovering just above the accretion disk that blasts out intense X-rays.
- **Particle Jets:** Massive beams of plasma and radiation shot out from the black hole's magnetic poles at nearly the speed of light (usually seen in supermassive black holes that are actively feeding).
